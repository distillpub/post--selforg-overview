Thread: Differentiable Self-organizing Systems

A living collection of articles and comments exploring an emerging research direction

Complex structures in nature form in a way that’s drastically different from those human’s create. Global order emerges from local interactions between tiny constituent particles -- a process called self-organization. These self-organizing systems can be remarkably flexible and robust. As a result, there’s been significant research interest in engineering self-organizing systems, but it remains an open research topic.

Differentiable programming seems like it could be a natural fit for this challenge. If backpropagation can optimize through many non-linear neural network layers, why shouldn’t it be able to optimize the local rules of a self-organizing system?
